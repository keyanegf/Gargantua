<h2 data-block-id="31f8fe09-7431-475e-98d6-f7924056b65a">Overview</h2>

<p data-block-id="608d07db-ddea-45e8-8887-1c70dad95616">Measuring attribution is becoming increasingly challenging and complex. As the privacy landscape evolves, shifting guidelines and regulations become more and more difficult to keep up with. As these rules grow and change, attribution solutions are becoming more intricate and harder to maintain.</p>

<p data-block-id="5d49da7c-3f90-4869-8be4-8fc81450d918"><img data-block-id="b73febc5-640b-4d11-839d-b77bd8ba0d81" src="https://files.us.document360.io/082e8be0-ad38-4650-9062-689a75e517a2/Images/Documentation/c62f76bd264597b1fd1fa6ac42e002c257115ef0732f48c29072b93de711e8d4-img2.png" class="adv-wysiwyg-img" mediatype="img" alt="" title="" width="280" height="auto" dataalign="right" datadisplay="inline" data-type="media-content" fixaspectratio="false" autoaspectratio="false" shadow="no" border="no" round="no" link="" newtab="" style=""></p>

<p data-block-id="9b4fbf47-9f60-4232-8fd4-ee6c08c0d64b">On top of all this, there is a growing number of opted-out users in marketing programs, lowering the accuracy of campaign performance measurements. This leaves you guessing as to how to optimize user engagement, especially among iOS users.</p>

<h2 data-block-id="4881e75d-0380-41f4-a68b-6a7bd5ca18e7">What Is PAM?</h2>

<p data-block-id="74258c3d-07e3-4d94-8113-5fa021f151d0"><strong>Predictive Aggregate Measurement (PAM)</strong> is an innovative approach to mobile campaign attribution, designed for performance marketers navigating today’s complex, privacy-focused mobile measurement world.</p>

<p data-block-id="e3cef117-3355-42a0-a652-9b0874317d7b">PAM is designed to navigate the complexities of today's changing privacy landscape while still expanding attribution coverage.</p>

<p data-block-id="a4cac143-fb5d-4246-84ae-d06048631563">With PAM, you can <strong>unlock additional insights for users previously marked as either</strong> <code name="">Unattributed</code> <strong>or</strong> <code name="">reported under SKAN</code>.</p>

<p data-block-id="fd5480c5-12f5-4750-a7ab-c290da5db97a">By utilizing our predictive modeling tools alongside strict privacy protocols, PAM helps safeguard user privacy while also providing marketers with the most granular and effective reporting methodology available for each unique situation.</p>

<p data-block-id="4a07ac58-9900-4067-b0cb-61af61c92bb9">Our dynamic models adjust to the data available, employing the most precise attribution methods for each situation. We do this with user privacy in mind, so marketers can focus on analyzing performance and optimizing their strategies.</p>

<h2 data-block-id="6f2cc06f-0a48-4ac7-883f-ed11dd045dc1">Data precautions</h2>

<p data-block-id="ce4bfb3a-12ac-4e53-8939-c12121cc93b9">PAM maximizes attribution coverage while still remaining privacy-focused.</p>

<p data-block-id="a948a7a3-e356-4981-a8be-76bb9aea0acf">Below are the methods PAM uses to help you meet your privacy compliance requirements.</p>

<h3 data-block-id="0f1143e4-cbf4-49e5-9aaf-da462cd26acd">Thresholding</h3>

<p data-block-id="aaf7657a-b3eb-4989-b2e1-2cca61c7629d">PAM also applies thresholding techniques to the data collected, which helps prevent individual user actions from being traced back to them across mobile apps and the web. In this way, PAM helps maintain user anonymity.</p>

<p data-block-id="13ccbd8f-b4c7-4f88-b00e-cb7e39207148">These techniques allow marketers to access valuable insights through aggregated data, without exposing individual user-level information.</p>

<h3 data-block-id="6daf9b26-7528-4db3-8930-ad0926345b31">Data aggregation</h3>

<p data-block-id="79815265-d66a-4a96-a158-d5248e6c3c2c">To further help protect privacy, PAM aggregates campaign data, bringing individual data points into a broader dataset.</p>

<p data-block-id="978e5f1e-30db-4220-96ba-5caff7f6fdad">This provides a comprehensive view of campaign performance but doesn't expose data about the individual user.</p>

<p data-block-id="1ef0b036-9f75-4fa2-9bfd-171cdf4c922c">The aggregated data is presented in a way that marketers can get actionable insights while still preventing user-level identification.</p>

<p data-block-id="2dcc656f-e87a-4ce6-989d-732b7611a07b">Together, these three data precautions help ensure that your user's privacy is respected, while still giving you valuable insights.</p>

<h2 data-block-id="md53hde4-ja9ves-006">PAM for Publishers</h2>

<p data-block-id="md6jmarh-igjzrq-010">The <strong>PAM for Publishers</strong> toggle is available in the <span contenteditable="false" translate="no" data-glossary="postback" data-glossary-name="postback" id="blockglossary" class="fr-deletable popover__title">{{glossary.postback}}</span> <a href="/v1/docs/basic-postback-configuration" target="_blank" translate="no">configuration</a> settings for each ad partner. It’s designed to help advertisers maintain strong performance while respecting user privacy.</p>

<p data-block-id="md6jmb6j-vimtds-012">When enabled, this feature removes advertiser-specific user identifiers (e.g., <code>user_data_xxx</code>) from <span contenteditable="false" translate="no" data-glossary="postbacks" data-glossary-name="postbacks" id="blockglossary" class="fr-deletable popover__title">{{glossary.postbacks}}</span> unless the user has opted in via Apple’s App Tracking Transparency (ATT). In doing so, it prioritizes identifiers provided directly by the ad partner—ensuring compliant, high-quality signals that support effective campaign optimization.</p>

<h2 data-block-id="572da3f3-5010-4057-aaeb-971343c1ec8f">FAQ</h2>

<ol data-block-id="21c43802-e4fa-4a36-a90c-abe0b0b29681" start="1"><li data-block-id="294480ae-44e9-41dc-9393-137326425776" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="2be29561-9a9a-4d6f-86c7-5a2f7210c343"><strong>Does Branch offer probabilistic matching?</strong><br type="inline"> Yes - Branch provides customers with both <a href="/v1/docs/predictive-modeling" target="_self">Predictive Modeling (PREM)</a> and Predictive Aggregated Measurement (PAM).</p></li><li data-block-id="813f275c-d4eb-459b-a779-56a6e9ed318e" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="e944d985-4cf2-400f-ac25-249e3af37807"><strong>What is required to implement PAM?</strong><br type="inline"> Implementing PAM doesn't require any engineering resources, and is an easy lift for marketers. Simply follow <a href="/v1/docs/predictive-aggregate-measurement#implementation" target="_self" translate="no">these implementation steps</a> to enable PAM at the org-level or app-level with a switch.</p></li><li data-block-id="ff2973cb-1994-44ec-9a86-f72e519e7627" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="8c9de8df-d6db-46dc-8eed-e579f494f5f3"><strong>What does Undisclosed mean in Branch Dashboard reports?</strong><br type="inline"> When data attributed via PAM does not meet privacy thresholds for the selected date range, Branch will display <code name="">Undisclosed</code> in the Dashboard. This threshold may be surpassed once campaign performance improves enough to reveal the numerical PAM attribution uplift.<br type="inline"><img data-block-id="d3810750-1728-41f0-8fd8-0130e2bf40ac" src="https://files.us.document360.io/082e8be0-ad38-4650-9062-689a75e517a2/Images/Documentation/06a0fa8c4d88458b8301da3effca881af60ca1162794e205ec397eefbebbe816-unnamed_1.png" class="adv-wysiwyg-img" mediatype="img" alt="" title="" width="1200" height="auto" dataalign="left" datadisplay="flex" data-type="media-content" fixaspectratio="false" autoaspectratio="false" shadow="no" border="yes" round="no" link="" newtab="" style=""></p></li><li data-block-id="3322b388-bbc2-4566-8d24-5f91448474c2" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="f0a331ca-6e1e-4523-af94-865964606a23"><strong>Is Branch reverting to modeled conversions?</strong><br type="inline"> Modeled conversions from Ad Networks will flow into the attribution waterfall of PAM.</p></li><li data-block-id="0cb768d6-1ad8-4061-a6c4-dff93ebb948c" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="a4fc8e0e-aa55-4629-9090-fcbb64a63a37"><strong>How will Branch dedupe this aggregate data against the log-level owned channel data?</strong><br type="inline"> PAM will utilize the deduplication logic we have with opt-in conversions and SKAN. The data will live within<br type="inline"> our Event-Level Reporting suite and be deduplicated against all other sources within our Unified View Analytics.</p></li><li data-block-id="b9f7c288-90aa-497f-9a78-c3b37e2202e2" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="59c10a5f-dabf-4a24-b170-c144650d5eb4"><strong>What do “Differential Privacy Controls” entail?</strong><br type="inline"> Differential Privacy Controls include various end-user privacy practices that neither rely on nor expose individual identifiers across apps and websites. Examples of Differential Privacy Controls include (but are not limited to): noise introduction, thresholding, etc.</p></li><li data-block-id="e4ef5f23-4055-4585-b3c3-02e2055e36d2" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="7ac0b928-10f3-4564-af40-c086f7047632"><strong>Can you consider a conversion deterministic if you’re using an aggregate ID?</strong><br type="inline"> Yes, if the aggregate ID is not individually identifying. This implies a many to one relationship, which can be pulled deterministically and referenced deterministically.</p></li><li data-block-id="8090d7fa-0a9a-45a0-a4c7-28e7cf6a4142" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="e738d987-8b5e-4d0b-8597-8d4bcf398f9b"><strong>Will this affect channels other than opted-out iOS users under paid ads?</strong><br type="inline"> No. PAM increases visibility for opted-out iOS users under paid ads without affecting SEO measurement or other channels. Users previously marked as either <code name="">Unattributed</code> or <code name="">reported under SKAN</code> will be available for additional insights under PAM.</p></li><li data-block-id="eaabe4b2-4247-45e6-9fb9-c92716466d73" style="--pseudoBefore--fontWeight:bold"><p data-block-id="679729e3-c2c1-4b77-840f-8ea9723f6c34"><strong>Is fingerprinting forbidden?</strong><br type="inline"> Yes, fingerprinting is forbidden. Branch does not use fingerprinting - we utilize probabilistic matching techniques.</p></li></ol>
